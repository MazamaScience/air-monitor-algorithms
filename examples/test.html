air-monitor-algorithms

A visual walk-through of three core functions

The air-monitor-algorithms package provides algorithms for analyzing hourly air quality time series. All timestamps are UTC Luxon DateTime objects, and missing values are represented as null.

This page generates a 336-hour (14-day) synthetic PM2.5 dataset for America/Los_Angeles, modeling a clean-air baseline, a daily (diurnal) cycle, and a multi-day wildfire-smoke event peaking around day 7. Charts are drawn with Chart.js.

NowCast vs. raw PM2.5

pm_nowcast(pm25) implements the US EPA NowCast algorithm: a 12-hour weighted rolling average that tracks rapid changes (such as the onset of a smoke event) far more responsively than a simple average, while smoothing hour-to-hour noise. The first hours are null until enough data has accumulated.

Daily statistics

dailyStats(datetime, pm25, timezone) aggregates each local calendar day (midnight-to-midnight in the given timezone) into min / mean / max. The rise and fall of the smoke event is clearly visible across the two weeks.

Diurnal profile (average day)

diurnalStats(datetime, pm25, timezone, 14) collapses all 14 days onto a single 24-hour profile by grouping observations by hour-of-day. This reveals the characteristic daily shape — here a mean that peaks in the morning, with the min/max band showing the spread between clean days and smoke-event days.
